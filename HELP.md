Welcome to the Expense Settlement App! This guide will walk you through all the features to help you manage and settle group expenses easily.

## üöÄ Getting Started

The app is divided into a **sidebar** for configuration and a **main area** for entering and viewing data.

---

## üë• 1. Configure People (Sidebar)

Before you can add expenses, you need to tell the app who is involved.

### Payer Names
-   **What it is**: A list of all the people who might pay for an expense.
-   **How to use**: Enter each person's name, separated by a comma.
-   **Example**: `Alice, Bob, Charlie`

### Participant Names
-   **What it is**: A list of all the people who might participate in (and therefore owe money for) an expense. This list can be the same as the payers or include additional people.
-   **How to use**: Enter each person's name, separated by a comma.
-   **Example**: `Alice, Bob, Charlie, David, Eve`

The app will automatically create dropdown menus based on these lists, making data entry fast and consistent.

---

## üí£ 2. Reset App (Sidebar)

-   If you want to start completely fresh, click the **Reset App** button.
-   **Warning**: This is a destructive action and will clear everything: all names, all entered expenses, and any calculated results.

---

## üìù 3. Add a New Expense (Main Area)

This is where you'll spend most of your time.

### Expense Sharing
First, decide how many ways the expense will be split.
-   Click **‚ûï Add Participant** to add a new person to the expense form.
-   Click **‚ûñ Remove Participant** to remove the last person from the form.
-   The **Sharing Between 'n' participants** metric shows you how many people are currently set to share the cost.

### Expense Details
Fill out the compact form for each expense:
-   **Expense Type**: A short description (e.g., "Dinner", "Groceries", "Movie Tickets").
-   **Amount**: The total cost of the expense (e.g., `25.50`).
-   **Payer**: Select the person who paid for this expense from the dropdown menu.

### Participants
This is where you specify who was part of the expense.
-   **Participant Name**: For each slot, select a person from the dropdown.
-   **Count**: This is the "head count". If a single participant entry represents more than one person (e.g., a couple or a family), you can set the count accordingly.
    -   **Example**: If "Alice" represents herself and a guest, you would select "Alice" and set her count to `2`.

Once the form is complete, click the green **Add Expense** button. The expense will appear in the "Entered Expenses" table below.

---

## üßæ 4. View and Manage Entered Expenses

This table shows a read-only list of all the expenses you've added.

### Deleting an Expense
If you make a mistake, you can easily delete an entry:
1.  Check the **Delete** box next to the row(s) you want to remove.
2.  A red **Delete selected expense(s)** button will appear. Click it to confirm the deletion.

---

## üìà 5. Understand the Live Financial Summary

This powerful table updates in real-time as you add or remove expenses. It gives you an at-a-glance overview of the financial situation.
-   **Total Paid**: The sum of all money paid by each person.
-   **Total Owed**: The total share of all expenses for each person.
-   **Difference**: The most important row.
    -   A **positive** number means that person is owed money.
    -   A **negative** number (in red) means that person owes money.
-   **Check**: This is a verification column. The totals here should always be zero (or very close to it), confirming that the money in and money out are balanced.

---

## üí≥ 6. Calculate the Final Settlement

Once all expenses are entered, you can generate the final payment plan.

1.  Click the green **‚úÖ Calculate Settlement** button.
2.  A **Settlement Plan** will appear, showing you the simplest way for everyone to clear their debts (e.g., "Charlie pays Alice: $15.50").
3.  To hide the plan, click the **üóëÔ∏è Reset Calculation** button. This does not delete any of your data; it only hides the settlement plan.
